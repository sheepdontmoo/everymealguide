# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Every Meal Guide is for normal consumers trying to choose a meal delivery service without spending hours reading affiliate blogs, Reddit threads, coupon pages, and brand websites. They may be parents, busy workers, fitness-focused shoppers, students, people trying prepared meals for convenience, or anyone comparing meal kits, ready meals, diet plans, frozen meals, grocery dinner shortcuts, and local meal-prep companies across their country.

## Product Purpose

The product exists to help a visitor answer "What meal delivery service should I use?" within 60 seconds of landing on the site. Brand coverage is the database, not the mission. The mission is fast, confident decision-making through clear top picks, country-aware recommendations, honest tradeoffs, comparison pages, review pages, offer checks, and transparent affiliate disclosures.

Success means the homepage explains the purpose in under five seconds, a visitor reaches a recommendation in under three clicks, every monetizable brand has a tracked `/go/` path, and the site builds enough trust and coverage to earn affiliate revenue without exposing internal operator jargon.

## Brand Personality

Helpful, sharp, and commercially honest. The voice should feel like a smart friend who has done the research, not a spreadsheet, coupon farm, or fake review site. It should be confident, plain-spoken, fast to scan, and clear about uncertainty.

## Anti-references

Do not look like an internal affiliate dashboard, raw database dump, coupon spam site, generic AI landing page, or old-school comparison table from 2008. Do not expose phrases like money route, cash route, partner route, affiliate approval pending, tracked route, ledger, source queue, or next action to consumers. Avoid overwhelming users with every row of the database before helping them choose.

## Design Principles

1. Choice first, database second.
2. Trust beats hype.
3. Every page should answer the next buying question.
4. Comparison volume must be framed as guidance, not clutter.
5. Affiliate mechanics must be disclosed clearly without becoming the content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast, keyboard navigation, focus states, and responsive layouts. Support reduced motion. Avoid tiny text, low-contrast muted copy, horizontal overflow, and mobile layouts that hide the recommendation path. Dietary and medical-diet content should avoid unsafe health claims and remind users to confirm suitability where needed.
