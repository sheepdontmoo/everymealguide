# Every Meal Guide Revenue Model

## Target

Reach at least **$5,000/month** from meal-delivery affiliate revenue.

This is a planning model, not a guarantee.

## Core Formula

```text
Monthly revenue =
monthly organic sessions
x affiliate click-through rate
x merchant conversion rate
x average commission
```

## Example Scenarios

| Scenario | Monthly sessions | Affiliate CTR | Merchant conversion | Avg commission | Monthly revenue |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Conservative | 25,000 | 8% | 3% | $35 | $2,100 |
| Base case | 40,000 | 10% | 4% | $35 | $5,600 |
| Strong | 60,000 | 12% | 4.5% | $40 | $12,960 |

## What Has To Be True For $5k/month

One plausible path:

1. 40,000 monthly organic sessions.
2. 10% of visitors click an affiliate CTA.
3. 4% of affiliate clicks convert at the merchant.
4. Average commission is around $35.

That means:

```text
40,000 sessions
x 10% = 4,000 affiliate clicks
x 4% = 160 conversions
x $35 = $5,600/month
```

## Practical Traffic Target

The first milestone is not $5k. The first milestone is evidence:

1. 1,000 monthly organic sessions.
2. 100 affiliate clicks.
3. At least one approved affiliate program.
4. At least one tracked conversion or merchant-side sale.

After that, scale pages that produce clicks.

## Biggest Revenue Levers

1. **Affiliate approvals**
   - Without approvals, traffic cannot monetize properly.

2. **Money-page rankings**
   - Best, deals, reviews, and vs pages matter more than broad guides.

3. **Affiliate click-through rate**
   - Better ranking cards, verdicts, deal warnings, and sticky CTAs improve this.

4. **Merchant conversion rate**
   - Higher-intent pages like `Factor vs CookUnity` should convert better than generic traffic.

5. **Commission quality**
   - Some brands/programs may pay more or less; track real values once approved.

## Page Types Most Likely To Drive Revenue

1. Deal pages.
2. Brand review pages.
3. Brand vs brand pages.
4. Best prepared meal pages.
5. Cheapest/budget pages.
6. Country pages only after availability is accurate.

## Early KPI Targets

| Stage | Sessions/month | Affiliate clicks/month | Approved programs | Revenue expectation |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Launch | 0-500 | 0-25 | 0-1 | $0 likely |
| Indexing | 500-2,500 | 25-200 | 1-3 | first clicks/sales possible |
| Validation | 2,500-10,000 | 200-1,000 | 3-5 | prove conversion path |
| Scale | 10,000-40,000 | 1,000-4,000 | 5+ | realistic path to $5k |

## What To Measure Weekly

1. Sessions by page.
2. Affiliate clicks by page.
3. Affiliate clicks by brand.
4. Search impressions by query.
5. Search clicks by page.
6. Approved programs.
7. Merchant-side conversions.
8. Average commission per conversion.

## If Revenue Is Weak

Use this diagnostic order:

1. No traffic:
   - Improve SEO depth and internal links.
   - Publish more P0/P1 pages.

2. Traffic but no affiliate clicks:
   - Improve page design, CTAs, verdicts, and deal framing.
   - Move CTA higher.

3. Clicks but no conversions:
   - Check brand fit.
   - Check offer quality.
   - Compare affiliate program landing page.
   - Test different brand priority.

4. Conversions but low revenue:
   - Prioritize higher commission programs.
   - Build more pages around brands that convert.

Use `seo/cro-backlog.md` for concrete tests when traffic exists but affiliate clicks are weak.

## Do Not Do

- Do not buy ads before affiliate tracking and approved links work.
- Do not publish low-intent recipes as a shortcut.
- Do not inflate offer claims to increase clicks.
- Do not judge the site before pages have time to index.
